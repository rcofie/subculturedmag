Upcoming Talent: Pattern

SUBCULTUREDNovember 6, 2019
Art & PhotographyMusic0 Comments

Last Friday, Guildford-based band – Pattern – played at the Face Bar in Reading, an alternative music venue loved by locals for its intimate space and display of new talent. The band played some of their singles including: ‘Can’t Sleep’, ‘L.A.T’ and ‘What You Do’, with the crowd enjoying their chilled and groovy vibe. With influences including icons such as Radiohead and Tom Misch, the band have created a sound that mixes rock, funk and pop with heavenly guitar solos and choruses that will resonate in your head for days on end. When asked how they would describe their sound, the band answered: “We wouldn’t really stick one genre onto our band. We mainly just like to write music that we think sounds cool, whether it be in a similar genre to the last song or something completely different.” 

The band, made up of singer Louis Whitley; guitarists Will Jones and George Fleming; bassist Jack Taylor and Max Colley on drums are all students from ACM in Guildford where they met and started playing together last year. With three songs already on Spotify and another due to come out any day now, the band are well on their way to being successful. Whilst discussing the song writing process, the band said; ‘Usually Will or Jack would start with a cool melody or bass line, and then Max would create a drum groove that works well with it. We usually experiment a lot.’ Coming up with original and meaningful lyrics can often be the most problematic part of writing music, which Pattern confirm; “Making different sections of songs and making sure the previous section works with the new section is the biggest challenge. But sometimes we just jam together and a cool idea or song will just come out of no-where.”  

In their short time together, they have become a real presence in the local music scene, with their talent recognised by BBC Introducing and played on BBC Radio Surrey. They have played venues like The Boileroom supporting Australian band: Planet, The Acoustic Lounge in Bracknell, The Basement in Kingston and The Star Inn, as well as The Tunbridge Wells Forum which they will have played at in October to support punk band Feet.  

Make sure to follow their Instagram: @patternofficial_ for all things Pattern and upcoming gigs.
